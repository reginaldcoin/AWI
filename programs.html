Overview of Drawing and Talking

Drawing and Talking is a structured, one-on-one intervention that allows individuals to process emotions and trauma by expressing themselves through drawing in a safe and supportive environment. This approach is grounded in fostering secure attachments, as a trained practitioner remains present and engaged, creating a consistent, non-judgmental space. It is particularly effective for individuals who find verbal expression challenging, enabling them to symbolically explore feelings and experiences at their own pace.

Training in this intervention is beneficial for a wide range of professionals who work with vulnerable groups, including teachers, social workers, mental health nurses, youth workers, and counselors. It equips practitioners with the skills to guide individuals through a 12-week process of emotional processing and growth. The training is accessible to those without prior formal qualifications in mental health or therapy, making it an inclusive option for individuals seeking to expand their ability to support others.

Organizations that would benefit from having trained employees include schools, children’s charities, elder care, women’s refuges, refugee support groups, and youth justice teams. These workplaces often interact with individuals who may have experienced trauma or emotional challenges, and this intervention provides a proactive, accessible method to help individuals find resolution and emotional balance.
